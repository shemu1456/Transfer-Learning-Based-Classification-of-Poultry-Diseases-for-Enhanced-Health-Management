Understanding Common Poultry Diseases

Posted on June 27, 2025 by Admin

Poultry farming is a vital agricultural sector, but it's constantly challenged by various diseases that can lead to significant economic losses. Early detection and proactive management are crucial for maintaining a healthy flock and ensuring sustainable production.

Some of the most common poultry diseases include:

  • Coccidiosis: A parasitic disease caused by protozoa that infect the intestinal tract, leading to diarrhea, weight loss, and mortality.
  • Newcastle Disease: A highly contagious viral disease affecting respiratory, nervous, and digestive systems. It can cause severe illness and high death rates.
  • Salmonellosis: A bacterial infection that can affect poultry and also be transmitted to humans. Symptoms in birds vary but can include diarrhea and reduced egg production.
  • Avian Influenza (Bird Flu): A highly pathogenic viral disease that can spread rapidly and cause severe illness and death in poultry.
  • Marek's Disease: A viral disease that causes tumors in various organs, including nerves and skin, leading to paralysis and immunosuppression.

Recognizing the symptoms of these diseases is the first step in effective management. Farmers should regularly monitor their birds for signs such as lethargy, ruffled feathers, reduced feed intake, respiratory distress, abnormal droppings, and swelling.
